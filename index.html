Famous Japanese Delights

Beyond our own creations, we love to appreciate the rich baking traditions from around the world. Here are some famous Japanese treats that are culinary masterpieces:

Mochi

A traditional Japanese rice cake made of mochigome (glutinous rice). It's pounded into a paste and molded into various shapes, often filled with sweet red bean paste or ice cream. Its chewy texture is unique!

Dorayaki

Two small pancake-like patties made from castella cake, wrapped around a filling of sweet azuki red bean paste. It's a popular snack, famously loved by the anime character Doraemon.

Melon Pan

A type of sweet bread from Japan, characterized by a thin, crisp cookie crust on top of a soft, fluffy bread. It's named for its resemblance to a melon, not for its flavor (it usually doesn't taste like melon).

Daifuku

Another variety of Mochi, Daifuku is a soft, chewy rice cake typically filled with sweet red bean paste (anko). It's often dusted with potato starch or cornstarch to prevent sticking.
